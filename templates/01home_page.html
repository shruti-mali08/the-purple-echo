BACKGROUND HISTORY

BTS, also known as Bangtan Sonyeondan (방탄소년단) or “Bulletproof Boy Scouts,” is a South Korean boy band formed by Big Hit Entertainment in 2013. The group debuted on June 13, 2013, with seven members—RM, Jin, Suga, J-Hope, Jimin, V, and Jungkook. Known for their socially conscious lyrics and authentic storytelling, BTS addresses issues such as mental health, societal pressures, and self-empowerment, allowing them to connect deeply with fans worldwide. Their music blends genres like pop, hip-hop, R&B, and EDM, while their performances are recognized for intricate choreography and emotional depth. Supported by their devoted fanbase, ARMY (Adorable Representative MC for Youth), BTS continues to inspire millions globally through albums such as Wings, the Love Yourself series, and Map of the Soul, spreading messages of growth, empathy, and self-love.
